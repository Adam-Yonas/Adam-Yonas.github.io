DeSCENT

Space Systems Design Studio

Alpha CubeSat is a 1U spacecraft developed through Cornell’s Space Systems Design Studio (SSDS), a student-led program founded by Professor Mason Peck focused on rapid, flight-driven spacecraft development. The project serves as a platform for designing, building, and validating real flight hardware, with an emphasis on deployable systems and end-to-end spacecraft integration. Within SSDS, Alpha CubeSat contributes to a broader effort to develop low-cost, flight-ready technologies that can be tested in real space environments, including ISS deployment.

Overview

DeSCENT is a ChipSat deployer developed through Cornell’s SSDS to enable controlled release of ultra-small spacecraft from a CubeSat platform. The system is designed to securely stow multiple ChipSats during launch and reliably deploy them on orbit, supporting SSDS’s broader efforts in distributed sensing and low-cost space systems. The project emphasizes simplicity, reliability, and compatibility with CubeSat integration and launch constraints.

Details

Mechanism design, fabrication, and testing.

Design

The deployer was designed as a compact, self-contained mechanism capable of housing and releasing ChipSats in a controlled manner. The system uses a burn-wire actuated release mechanism paired with a spring-loaded deployment system to ensure separation from the host spacecraft. Design work focused on packaging within tight volume constraints, ensuring positive retention during launch, and achieving clean, repeatable deployment without interference or jamming.

Analysis

Key analyses focused on ensuring the mechanism would survive launch loads and function reliably in space. This included evaluating retention forces, spring sizing, and release timing to achieve consistent deployment velocities. Thermal and electrical considerations were also addressed for the burn-wire system, ensuring reliable actuation after exposure to vibration and thermal vacuum environments.

Manufacturing

The deployer was fabricated using a combination of machined components and rapid prototyping to enable fast iteration. Assembly required precise integration of mechanical components, springs, and the burn-wire system within a compact structure. Multiple iterations were produced to refine tolerances, improve reliability, and ensure consistent performance across builds.

Testing / Results

DeSCENT was validated through functional and environmental testing, including repeated deployment tests to verify reliability and consistency. The burn-wire mechanism was characterized to ensure predictable actuation, and the system was tested under conditions representative of launch and space environments. Results demonstrated reliable ChipSat release and informed further design improvements for future iterations.
