Cornell Mars Rover Suspension

Cornell Mars Rover

The Cornell Mars Rover is a student-built system designed for the University Rover Challenge (URC), focused on reliable traversal across rough, uneven terrain. The rover emphasizes mobility, durability, and rapid iteration under real-world field conditions.

Overview

I worked on the Drives and Suspension subteam, focusing on rover mobility through suspension geometry and drivetrain integration. My work centered on adapting a JPL-style rocker-bogie suspension to our rover while developing an alternative bevel gear drivetrain to improve torque transmission and packaging within constrained wheel assemblies.

  • Rocker-bogie suspension adapted for competition rover constraints
  • Bevel gear drivetrain concept for improved torque routing
  • Integrated suspension and drivetrain within tight packaging limits
  • Designed for uneven terrain traversal and load distribution
My Role: Suspension Subteam Lead (Nov 2020 - Sep 2023)
  • Suspension CAD design and geometry development
  • Integration of drivetrain components within rocker-bogie system
  • Development of bevel gear drivetrain architecture
  • Structural analysis (FEA + hand calculations)
  • Manufacturing support and mechanical assembly
Details

Mobility system design and implementation.

Suspension Architecture

The rover uses a rocker-bogie suspension to maintain wheel contact across uneven terrain while distributing loads across all six wheels. The system was adapted from JPL architectures and modified to meet competition constraints, including weight limits, manufacturability, and packaging.

  • Rocker-bogie geometry tuned for stability and articulation
  • Maintained wheel-ground contact over large obstacles
  • Balanced load distribution across all wheels
Drivetrain Design

In addition to standard drivetrain integration, I developed a bevel gear-based drivetrain concept to improve torque transmission and simplify packaging within wheel assemblies. This approach enabled more compact motor placement and cleaner power routing.

  • Bevel gear drivetrain for torque redirection into wheel hubs
  • Improved packaging within constrained suspension geometry
  • Reduced complexity of shaft routing
Integration Challenges

A major challenge was integrating drivetrain components within the moving suspension system while maintaining alignment and minimizing losses. This required careful coordination between suspension geometry and drivetrain layout.

  • Aligned drivetrain within moving rocker-bogie joints
  • Maintained tolerances across dynamic interfaces
  • Balanced robustness with serviceability
Analysis

Structural and kinematic analyses were used to validate suspension performance under terrain-induced loads. This included evaluating stress in suspension members and ensuring drivetrain components could handle expected torque requirements.

  • FEA on suspension components
  • Hand calculations for load paths and torque requirements
  • Trade studies on drivetrain configurations
Manufacturing & Assembly

Hardware was fabricated and assembled with a focus on rapid iteration. Components were designed for manufacturability and ease of replacement during testing and competition.

  • Machining and fabrication support
  • Mechanical assembly of suspension and drivetrain
  • Iterative builds based on testing feedback
Outcome

The resulting system provided a robust mobility platform capable of handling uneven terrain while maintaining stability and traction. The integration of drivetrain and suspension improved overall rover performance and informed future design iterations.

  • Functional rocker-bogie mobility system
  • Improved drivetrain packaging and torque delivery
  • Validated through field testing and iteration
