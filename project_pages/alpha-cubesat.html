Project

Alpha CubeSat

Space Systems Design Studio

Alpha CubeSat is a 1U spacecraft developed through Cornell’s Space Systems Design Studio (SSDS) as a flight-ready platform for a free-flying light sail technology demonstration. The mission combined in-house manufacturing, physical integration, and environmental validation to build a CubeSat within strict deployer interface constraints.

Overview

Alpha CubeSat served as a flight hardware program focused on manufacturing deployer-interface components, performing physical fit checks, and supporting assembly and environmental validation. Work centered on ensuring compatibility with the NanoRacks CubeSat Deployer and maintaining strict dimensional requirements.

  • 1U CubeSat platform with deployer-constrained interfaces
  • Rail hardware produced for flight, EDU, and spare sets
  • Fit-checked against NRCSD dimensional requirements
  • Integrated and validated through assembly and testing
My Role
  • Precision machining and fabrication of CubeSat rail hardware
  • Fabrication of deployment mechanism hardware (burn-wire latch system)
  • Dimensional verification and deployer interface fit checks
  • Physical assembly and mechanical integration support
  • Environmental testing support during validation
Details

Manufacturing, integration, and validation.

Manufacturing & Fabrication

Manufactured CubeSat rail interfaces used for integration with the NanoRacks CubeSat Deployer (NRCSD). Work required translating CAD geometry into machining operations while maintaining tight dimensional control.

  • Machined rail sets for flight, EDU, and spare hardware
  • Performed stock prep, slot milling, drilling, and tapping
  • Used 1/4 in and 1/8 in tooling for final geometry
  • Prepared components for anodization and revalidation
Deployment Mechanism Hardware

Machined a burn-wire latch mechanism used to retain the solar sail prior to deployment. The latch holds tension through a burn wire and releases upon actuation, enabling controlled deployment.

  • Machined latch component for burn-wire retention system
  • Designed for controlled release upon wire failure
  • Performed slot milling, drilling, and countersinking
  • Maintained tight tolerances on small-scale aluminum geometry
  • Produced test-ready hardware with flight-geometry intent
Precision & Tolerancing

Maintained deployer-compatible geometry while working across metric CAD and imperial tooling. Fabrication required iterative dimension control and accounting for post-processing effects.

  • Maintained CubeSat dimensions below 100.6 mm requirement
  • Developed repeatable 1 mm corner-rounding process
  • Worked within ~0.1 mm machining tolerances
  • Accounted for anodization thickness in final fit checks
Assembly & Integration

Supported physical assembly and interface validation of CubeSat hardware through repeated fit checks and integration of rail and switch interfaces.

  • Integrated rails onto CubeSat for fit verification
  • Validated switch actuation through assembled interfaces
  • Supported mechanical assembly of flight configurations
  • Verified deployer compatibility using CAD and physical checks
Environmental & Physical Validation

Supported validation flow to ensure hardware readiness for flight-like conditions through repeated fit checks and environmental testing support.

  • Performed repeated dimensional fit checks
  • Supported vibration and environmental testing readiness
  • Verified assembly quality and hardware accessibility
  • Contributed to multi-stage validation approach
Outcome

Delivered deployer-compatible hardware and supported integration and validation of a flight-ready CubeSat system.

  • Produced rail and latch hardware for testing and integration
  • Verified compliance with deployer interface constraints
  • Supported assembly and environmental validation
